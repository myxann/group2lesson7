Filipino Women in the Workforce

Published on February 21, 2025

Filipino women have joined the labor force due to economic necessity and opportunities. Despite growth in numbers and participation, women still face challenges in achieving employment parity with men.

Labor force participation remains lower than men's rates. Family responsibilities often constrain employment opportunities.

Female Labor Force Participation (1990 - 2019)

Published on February 21, 2025

Source: TheGlobalEconomy.com The World Bank

The table shows a data for the Philippines from 1990 to 2019. The data shows that the Female labor force participation averages 47.76% (1990 - 2019). The maximum rate was 49.52% in 2014 and with 45.42% minimum in 2017. The latest rate as of 2019 is 46.1%.

The World average in 2019 accross 182 countries in total is 51.98%.
